Traditional Clothing of Mussoorie

Traditional Clothing for Women
  • Sari: The sari is a timeless attire worn by women in Mussoorie and across India. It is a graceful and elegant drape that varies in style, fabric, and design.
  • Ghagra Choli: This is a traditional two-piece attire consisting of a long skirt (ghagra) paired with a blouse (choli) and a dupatta.
  • Odhani: The odhani is a decorative scarf or shawl that is often draped over the head or shoulders, complementing traditional outfits.
  • Angarkha: The angarkha is a traditional upper garment characterized by its overlapping panels and tied closures, commonly worn in North India.
Traditional Clothing for Men
  • Dhoti-Kurta: The dhoti-kurta ensemble is a classic attire for men. It consists of a draped lower garment (dhoti) paired with a tunic (kurta).
  • Pahadi Topi: The pahadi topi is a traditional cap worn by men in the hills of Uttarakhand. It is often made of wool and features distinctive patterns.
  • Bandi (Jacket): The bandi is a waist-length jacket, usually worn over a kurta. It adds a layer of warmth and style to the traditional attire.
  • Turban: The turban is a cultural headgear worn by men in various regions of India, including Mussoorie, as a symbol of pride and tradition.
  • Jootis: Jootis are traditional handcrafted shoes made of leather or fabric. They are comfortable footwear worn by men on various occasions.
